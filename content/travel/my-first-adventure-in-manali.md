## The Journey Begins

There's something about leaving the city at 10 PM on a Friday that feels like an act of rebellion. The overnight bus from Delhi rattled through the darkness, and I woke up to pine trees and the distant gleam of snow.

Manali greeted me with cold air that stung my lungs and a sky so blue it looked photoshopped. I dropped my bag at a small homestay in Old Manali and walked straight to the river.

## Old Manali & the Manu Temple

The narrow lanes of Old Manali have a charm you can't manufacture. Tiny cafes with mismatched furniture, prayer flags fluttering from rooftops, and the sound of the Beas River always in the background.

I visited the Manu Temple — believed to be the spot where the sage Manu meditated. It's a simple stone structure, but standing there with the valley spread below felt meditative in itself.

## Solang Valley

The next morning I took a shared cab to Solang Valley. The snow was thick and untouched in places. I tried my hand at skiing (poorly) and spent most of the time just sitting on a rock, watching clouds roll through the valley.

> "The mountains are calling, and I must go." — John Muir

## Chai by the River

The best part of any mountain trip isn't the tourist spots — it's the unplanned moments. I found a small shack by the river that served ginger chai in steel glasses. I sat there for two hours, reading a book, listening to water crash over rocks.

## Takeaways

- **Pack layers.** The weather changes every hour.
- **Stay in Old Manali**, not the main town. It's quieter and has more character.
- **Don't over-plan.** The best moments happen when you're wandering without a destination.
- **Talk to locals.** They know trails and viewpoints that Google Maps doesn't.

---

This trip reminded me why I travel — not for Instagram photos, but for the feeling of being small in a big, beautiful world.
